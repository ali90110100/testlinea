How The Mechanism Works

1

Network Fees

Users pay transaction fees on Linea in $ETH, which are stored in the fee contract.

2

Fee Distribution

The contract splits fees: 80% to burn $LINEA and 20% to burn $ETH.

3

Token Swap & Bridge

The 80% $ETH is swapped to $LINEA and bridged to Ethereum mainnet.

4

Token Burning

$LINEA and $ETH are sent to burn addresses, permanently removing them from circulation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is burning tokens important?

Token burning reduces the total supply of a cryptocurrency, which can increase scarcity and potentially drive up value if demand remains constant or increases. It's a deflationary mechanism that benefits long-term holders.

How often does the burn happen?

The burn mechanism operates continuously as transactions occur on the Linea network. Fees are collected and distributed according to the 80/20 split in real-time.

Where can I see the burn transactions?

You can track burn transactions on blockchain explorers like Etherscan for Ethereum mainnet burns and LineaScan for Linea network burns. The contract addresses are provided in the Contracts section above.

What is the maximum supply of $LINEA?

$LINEA started with an initial total supply of 72,009,990,000 tokens. There is no fixed maximum supply, but the continuous burning mechanism makes it deflationary over time.
